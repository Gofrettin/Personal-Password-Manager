Introduction to the Kryloff Personal Password Manager™

The Kryloff Personal Password Manager is developed by Sergey Kryloff and licensed you you under the GNU General Public license. It keeps a collection of your passwords locally in the encrypted form, and allows you to easily enter them into desired Web pages, applications or documents without using that insecure Windows clipboard.

The general process of entering your passwords looks as follows:
  • You first, enter your login and password into the Password Manager. This is required only once as Password Manager subsequently stores your passwords (in the encrypyted form).
  • Then, you open a Web page, an application or a document, and select a desired field to enter the password into.
  • Finally, you activate Password Manager, double click a cell, after which your password gets entered into the desired field. The Password Manager window caption indicates into which field the password is about to be entered.
